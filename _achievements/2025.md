<h3><strong>Achievements 2025</strong></h3>
<p><strong>International Science Drama Competition 2025 (Singapore Finals)</strong>
</p>
<p>We are pleased to announce that our school’s Drama Club CCA clinched the <strong>First Prize in the International Science Drama Competition 2025 (Singapore Finals), Junior Category</strong> organised
by Science Centre Singapore.</p>
<p>Our Drama team represented Singapore in the ISDC – Grand Finals (Junior
Category) which was held at Science Centre Singapore on Sun 03 Aug. 39
family members were also present at the competition to cheer them on. Our
team put up an inspiring performance titled “The Power Surge” to bring
across the importance of sustainable living using renewable energy and
impressed the judges and audience. They emerged <strong>Third in the competition</strong>! <strong>Chia Li Yen (6D)</strong> was
also awarded the <strong>Outstanding Performer Award</strong>!</p>
<p>We would like to congratulate the team on their outstanding achievement
and doing their personal best throughout the months of preparation. We
would also like to extend our heartfelt appreciation to parents and other
family members for the invaluable support and partnership in developing
our pupils to their fullest potential.</p>
<p>Congratulations to the Drama Club members!</p>

<p><strong>16th GoodTalk (Global Village) International Children &amp; Youth Bilingual Speech Conference </strong>
<br><strong>第 16 届 GoodTalk (地球村) 国际青少年双语演讲大会</strong>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;The conference aims to provide an opportunity for pupil participants
from Singapore and China to enhance their bilingual abilities and cultivate
their global competence. In addition, the conference aims to support the
Speak Mandarin Campaign in Singapore. The topic was “Century (百年)”. A century
is not only a mark of time, but also a witness to history and the inheritance
of culture. This topic coincides with our school’s centennial celebration,
where participants could trace back the century, looking at the present
and to the future. Our heartiest congratulations to the following pupils
for their achievements in the Preliminary Round!</p>

<p><strong>2025 Art Olympics International Illustration Competition</strong>
</p>
<p>The 2025 Art Olympics International Illustration Competition, organised
by the Organising Committee of International Youth Art (OCIYAC), focuses
on the theme “Climate Change”. Artists are invited to inspire the public
to change their lifestyles to more sustainable ones through their artworks.
Selected works will be exhibited at the Louvre in Paris. We are proud to
share that the artwork of <strong>Jarius Ong (5G) </strong>has won the <strong>Gold Award</strong> in
the competition!</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>2025 Hans Christian Andersen (International) Art Awards</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>Founded in Odense, Denmark, this award is organised
by the Denmark H.C. Andersen Culture &amp; Art Committee and the H.C. Andersen
Society. The 2025 edition of this award is themed “Friendship” and is open
to a broad spectrum of creators — from young children to adult artists.
We are pleased to share that <strong>Jarius Ong (5G)</strong> has achieved
the <strong>Silver Award</strong> in the Primary School Category. His work,
titled “Space Adventure: Soda VS Cactus Monster”, will be exhibited at
the Hans Christian Andersen International Art Award Exhibition in Odense
from 11 – 24 Aug. Well done, Jarius!</p>

Singapore Art Museum (SAM), Singaporeans are invited to reflect on what
Singapore means to them and share unique experiences in their everyday
lives. We are pleased to share that the artwork of <strong>Chen Xuanru (6D)</strong> was
