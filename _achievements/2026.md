<h3><strong>Achievements 2026</strong></h3>
<p><strong>International Science Drama Competition (ISDC) 2026 Singapore Finals</strong>
</p>
<p>Fifteen of our pupils proudly represented the school at the International
Science Drama Competition (ISDC) 2026 Singapore Finals on Sat 25 Apr. ISDC
is an annual platform that brings science to life through the performing
arts, challenging students to communicate complex ideas with clarity and
creativity. This year’s theme, “AI See the Future”, invited participants
to explore the principles, possibilities, and limitations of Artificial
Intelligence, while reflecting on its impact on society. Through a compelling
and thought-provoking performance titled “Granny.exe: The Butter Byte”,
our team demonstrated both scientific understanding and theatrical excellence
and emerged as champions in the Junior Category. <strong>They have earned the honour of representing Singapore at the 2026 ISDC Grand Finals (Junior Category) in Beijing, China, where they will compete against top teams from participating countries!</strong>
</p>

<p><strong>Muthamizh Vizha (Annual Tamil Literary Festival)</strong>
</p>
<p>The Muthamizh Vizha is an annual Tamil literary festival in Singapore,
organised by the Association of Singapore Tamil Writers and supported by
the Tamil Language Learning and Promotion Committee. The festival regularly
features an Oratory Competition designed specifically for Primary 5 pupils
to help build their confidence, vocabulary and Tamil public speaking skills.</p>

an award for reaching the Qualifying Round. Congratulations to Sushil Shyam

<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;<strong>Boys’ Brigade JM Fraser Award for Excellence (High Distinction)</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>We are pleased to share that the <strong>Boys’ Brigade (BB) 5J Singapore Company</strong> has
been awarded the <strong>JM Fraser Award for Excellence (High Distinction)</strong>!
This award recognises BB companies in Singapore for their high standards
in delivering programmes and activities that focus on holistic character
development. In attaining the High Distinction, BB 5J has demonstrated
excellence across areas such as 1 administration, stakeholder engagement,
capacity building and fund-raising, enrolment and membership, programme
delivery, as well as development and learning.</p>
<p>&nbsp;This achievement would not have been possible without the strong
support of our stakeholders. We remain committed to sustaining this standard
of excellence and providing meaningful experiences that continue to develop
our boys in character and leadership.</p>
<p><strong>Boys' Brigade (BB) Gold Award</strong>
</p>
<p>The Gold Award is the highest honour a BB Boy can attain in the Juniors
Programme. It is presented to Primary 6 boys who have demonstrated consistent
commitment through strong parade attendance, participation in key HQ-organised
BB programmes, completion of community service, and attainment of the required
colour awards. This year, we are proud to share that 14 of our boys were
presented with The Boys’ Brigade Gold Award. We commend them for their
dedication and perseverance throughout their BB journey!</p>

<p><strong>Boys’ Brigade Adventure Quest (Gold Honour Roll)</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong>BB AQ 2026 was held on Sat 28 Mar at Valour Primary
School and Punggol Waterway Park. A total of 516 Boys from 37 BB Companies
participated in the event. We are pleased to share that BB 5J was awarded
the Gold Honour Roll, with one of our four teams emerging among the Top
10 teams overall. BB AQ promotes characteristics of adventure, teamwork,
endurance, fighting spirit and sportsmanship among the BB boys. Through
the various challenges, the boys were stretched beyond their comfort zones
and given opportunities to build confidence and resilience. They also learnt
to take on leadership roles, support one another as team members, and cheer
one another on throughout the competition. The boys were tested across
multiple domains, including first aid and knot-tying, as well as adventure
challenges such as bicycle riding, tent pitching and a race-against-the-clock
obstacle course. It was no easy feat, and we are very proud of our boys
for their determination and perseverance. Well done, boys!</p>
